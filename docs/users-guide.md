# EventLineup — Users Guide

*Plan the lineup. Run the night.*

Welcome to **EventLineup**, the tool for planning DJ lineups and running event nights. This guide walks through everything the app can do, in plain language. No technical background needed — if you can use a calendar and a spreadsheet, you can use EventLineup.

This guide is written for **DJ-school teachers and staff** who organize events, manage a roster of student and graduate DJs, and want to share polished schedules with DJs and the public.

---

## Who uses EventLineup?

There are three kinds of people who touch the app, and each sees something different:

| Who | What they do | How they get in |
|-----|--------------|-----------------|
| **You (teacher / organizer)** | Build events, schedule DJs, manage your roster, track certifications | Sign in with Google |
| **Your DJs** | View their own set times, confirm they can make it, grab a promo graphic | A private link you send them — no login |
| **The public** | View the event schedule, see who's playing when | A public link you share — no login |

You are always in control. DJs and the public only ever see what you choose to share.

## Getting started

### Signing in

1. Go to the EventLineup login page.
2. Click **Continue with Google** and choose your account.
3. You'll land in your **Events** workspace — this is your home screen.

> You may also see an email field with a "Send magic link" button marked *Coming soon*. That's a future sign-in option and isn't active yet — use **Continue with Google** for now.

To leave, click **Sign out** in the top bar.

### Switching languages (English / 中文)

EventLineup is fully bilingual — **English** and **Traditional Chinese (中文)**. Use the **EN / 中文** toggle in the top bar at any time. Every screen, button, and label switches instantly, and your choice is remembered the next time you visit. This applies to the public schedule and DJ pages too, so DJs and audiences can read in whichever language they prefer.

### Finding your way around

Once you're signed in, every page shares the same layout:

- **Top bar** — the EventLineup brand, the language toggle, and **Sign out**.
- **Left sidebar ("Workspace")** — two main sections: **Events** and **DJs**. The one you're on is highlighted. Your organization name shows at the bottom.
- **Page header** — the page title, a search box, and a context-sensitive **+ New** button.

The two things you'll spend your time in are **Events** (planning nights) and **DJs** (managing your roster).

## Managing your DJ roster

Open **DJs** from the sidebar. This is where you keep track of everyone who plays at your events — and, for a school, where you track their progress.

### Adding a DJ

At the top of the DJ page there's an **Add a DJ** card:

1. Enter the DJ's **Name**.
2. Enter their **Genres**, comma-separated (for example: `House, Techno`).
3. Click **Add**.

They'll appear in the roster table below.

### The roster table

Each DJ row shows:

- **Name**
- **Genre tags** — what they play
- **Certifications** — a violet **Graduate** badge if they've graduated, plus a green badge for each genre they're certified in. Students with no certifications yet show *"None yet."*
- **Actions** — edit, copy their private portal link, and delete

### Editing a DJ and tracking certifications

Click the **Edit** (pencil) button on a DJ's row. A panel slides in from the right where you can manage everything about that DJ:

- **Name** — update it.
- **Status** — toggle between **Student** and **Graduate**. This is built for schools tracking a DJ's progression through the program.
- **Certifications** — tap the genre buttons to mark which parts of the curriculum a DJ has passed: **House, Hip Hop, Pop, Techno, Trance, Drum & Bass, R&B**. Selected ones show a checkmark.
- **Add a custom genre** — a free-text field for any certification beyond the standard list. These become removable tags.

Click **Save** to keep your changes, or **Cancel** to discard them.

> **Why certifications matter:** they're not just record-keeping. When you later schedule a DJ to play a genre they *aren't* certified for, EventLineup shows a gentle warning so you can make an informed choice (more on that below).

### Sending a DJ their private link

On any DJ's row, click the **Copy portal link** (link icon). It flips to a checkmark and says *"Copied!"* — the private link to that DJ's personal schedule is now on your clipboard. Send it to them however you like (LINE, email, etc.). See [The DJ portal](#the-dj-portal) for what they'll see.

### Removing a DJ

Click **Delete** (trash icon). You'll be asked to confirm *"Delete this DJ?"* before anything is removed.

## Creating and managing events

Open **Events** from the sidebar. This is your home base.

### The events list

At the top, three tiles give you a quick pulse on your calendar:

- **Upcoming events**
- **Past events**
- **Total events**

Use the **Upcoming / Past** tabs to filter the table below. Each event row shows its name (with a colored status dot), venue, dates, genres, and a status — a green **Live** badge appears when an event is happening right now.

Every row has quick **icon buttons**:

- **Edit** (pencil) — change the event's details.
- **Clone** (copy) — duplicate the event.
- **Delete** (trash) — remove it (shown on upcoming events).

### Creating a new event

Click **+ New Event** and fill in the form:

- **Event name**
- **Venue name**
- **Start date**
- **End date** (optional — leave it blank for a single-day event)
- **Genres** — comma-separated, e.g. `Hip Hop, R&B, Trap`

Click **Create**, or **Cancel** to go back.

### Cloning an event (great for recurring nights)

If you run a weekly or monthly night, you don't have to rebuild it each time. Click **Clone** on an event and confirm. EventLineup creates a fresh copy **with the same stages but no slots** — so your stage setup is ready and you just drop in the new lineup. You'll see *"Event cloned successfully."*

### Deleting an event

Click **Delete** and confirm. Note that deleting an event also removes **all its stages and slots**, so you'll be asked to confirm.

## Building the schedule (the heart of EventLineup)

Click any event to open its **detail page** — this is where you build the lineup. At the top you'll see the event name, its dates and venue, and genre "pills."

A row of action buttons sits in the header:

- **Back** — return to the events list.
- **View** (eye) — open the public schedule as your audience will see it.
- **Share via LINE** — send the schedule to LINE.
- **Export .xlsx** — download the schedule as an Excel spreadsheet.

### Setting up stages

A **stage** is a place where DJs play (a room, a booth, a deck). Stages appear as colored chips near the top of the page.

- Click **+ New Stage**, enter a **Stage name**, pick a **color** from the swatches, and click **Create**.
- To remove a stage, click the **×** on its chip and confirm. (Deleting a stage also removes its slots.)

Colors help you tell stages apart at a glance, especially in the grid view.

### Scheduling DJs into time slots

Below the stages is the **slots table** — this is your lineup, one row per set.

For multi-day events, **date tabs** let you switch between days. A **search box** helps you find a slot quickly, and you can **sort** by Stage, DJ, or Start time by clicking those column headers.

To **add a set**, use the inline **+ New Slot** row:

1. Pick a **Stage**.
2. Pick a **DJ** (or leave it as *"— unassigned —"* to block out time first).
3. Choose a **Genre**.
4. Set the **Start time** and **Duration** (handy preset durations are suggested).
5. Add any **Notes**.
6. Save the row (pressing **Enter** saves, **Esc** cancels).

To **edit** an existing set, click the pencil on its row — it becomes editable in place. To **remove** one, click the trash icon and confirm.

### Built-in warnings that keep you out of trouble

EventLineup watches for scheduling problems as you work and warns you in amber:

- **DJ double-booked** — *"{DJ} is already booked at this time."* The same DJ can't be in two places at once.
- **Stage overlap** — *"{Stage} already has a set at this time."* Two sets can't run on the same stage simultaneously.
- **Certification warning** — if you assign a DJ to a genre they're not certified for, that DJ shows a **⚠** in the dropdown and you'll see *"{DJ} isn't certified for {genre} — assign anyway to override."*

The certification warning is a **soft** warning — you can always override it. The double-booking and overlap checks protect the integrity of the schedule.

### Confirmation status at a glance

Each DJ's name in the slot table carries a badge showing whether they've responded to their booking:

- **✓ Confirmed** (green) — the DJ confirmed they can make it.
- **⚠ Flagged** (amber) — the DJ flagged a problem.
- **— Awaiting** (grey) — no response yet.

These badges update automatically when DJs respond through their portal (see below).

### Exporting to Excel

Click **Export .xlsx** to download a clean, formatted spreadsheet of the whole lineup — named after your event (e.g. `Summer-Bash-schedule.xlsx`). It has a titled header, and rows sorted by date, then stage, then start time, with columns for Date, Stage, Time Slot, DJ, Genre, and Notes. The column labels come out in whichever language you've selected. This is great for printing, emailing, or keeping a record.

## The DJ portal

Each DJ gets their own **private schedule page**. You give them access by copying their portal link from the DJ roster (the **Copy portal link** button) and sending it to them. They **don't need an account or password** — the link is the key.

When a DJ opens their link, they see:

- A header reading **"Your Schedule,"** their name, and their genre tags.
- All their sets, **grouped by event**. Each card shows the date, time, stage, genre, and any notes you added.

For each set, the DJ can:

- **Confirm** — a green check that tells you they're good to go. Once confirmed, the button locks.
- **Flag conflict** — let you know there's a problem.
- **Share my card** — open a polished promo graphic for that set (see below).

Whatever the DJ chooses flows straight back to your slot table as the **Confirmed / Flagged** badges, so you always know who's locked in.

> If a DJ's link ever stops working, they'll see *"This link has expired or is invalid. Ask your event organizer for a new link."* Just copy a fresh link from the roster and resend it. (Links are valid for about 90 days, and generating a new one retires the old one.)

## Sharing your event

EventLineup gives you several ways to get your lineup in front of people.

### The public schedule

Anyone with the public link can view the event schedule — no login. Open it yourself with the **View** (eye) button on the event page. It shows:

- The event name and venue.
- A **Share via LINE** button.
- **Date tabs** for multi-day events.
- On phones, a **stage-by-stage card view**; on larger screens, a **time-grid** with stages down the side and times across the top, each set shown as a colored block.

The language toggle works here too, so your audience can read in English or Chinese.

### Share via LINE

The **Share via LINE** buttons (on the event page and the public schedule) open LINE's share composer with the link ready to go. Sharing is always something **you choose to do** with a button — EventLineup never sends messages on its own.

### Promo "set cards"

For each individual set, EventLineup can generate a polished, shareable **set card** — a 4:5 promo graphic styled to the genre, featuring the DJ's name, stage, date, time, event, and venue. DJs can pull this up from their portal, or you can open it from a slot.

On a set card you can:

- **Share to LINE**
- **Save image** — download it as a PNG to post anywhere
- **Copy link** — grab the shareable link
- **View full lineup** — jump to the public schedule

These are perfect for social posts hyping up who's playing.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do my DJs need to create accounts?**
No. You send each DJ a private link, and that's all they need to view their schedule and confirm.

**Can the public edit anything?**
No. Public and DJ pages are view-and-confirm only. Editing the lineup is something only you (signed in) can do.

**What happens if I try to book a DJ twice at the same time?**
EventLineup warns you with an amber message and won't let conflicting sets stand on the same stage. You stay in control of how to resolve it.

**Can I override a certification warning?**
Yes. Certification warnings are advisory — assign the DJ anyway if you decide it's the right call.

**Is the app available in Chinese?**
Yes — full Traditional Chinese (中文) support across every screen, switchable any time from the top bar.

**Can I print or email the schedule?**
Yes — use **Export .xlsx** for a clean spreadsheet, or share the public link / LINE message.
