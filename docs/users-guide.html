Get started

Signing in & finding your way

Sign in with Google

  1. Open the EventLineup login page.
  2. Click Continue with Google and pick your account.
  3. You land in your Events workspace — your home screen.

A "Send magic link" email option may appear marked Coming soon — it isn't active yet. Use Google for now. To leave, click Sign out in the top bar.

Switch languages anytime EN / 中文

EventLineup is fully bilingual — English and Traditional Chinese (中文). Use the toggle in the top bar; every screen, button, and label switches instantly, and your choice is remembered next visit.

It applies to the public schedule and DJ pages too, so DJs and audiences can read in whichever language they prefer.

Getting around

  • Top bar — brand, language toggle, sign out.
  • Left sidebar — your two sections: Events and DJs.
  • Page header — title, search box, and a + New button.

Your roster

DJ roster & certifications

Open DJs from the sidebar to track everyone who plays — and, for a school, to track their progress through the curriculum.

Add a DJ

Use the Add a DJ card at the top: enter a Name, their Genres (comma-separated, e.g. House, Techno), and click Add. They appear in the roster table below.

Track certifications & progress

Click Edit (pencil) on a DJ — a panel slides in from the right where you manage everything:

  • Status — toggle Graduate vs Student to track progression through the program.
  • Certifications — tap genre buttons to mark passed curriculum: House, Hip Hop, Pop, Techno, Trance, Drum & Bass, R&B. Certified genres show as ✓ House badges.
  • Add a custom genre — a free-text field for certifications beyond the standard list.
Why it matters: certifications aren't just record-keeping. When you later schedule a DJ for a genre they aren't certified for, EventLineup gives you a gentle, override-able warning.

Send a DJ their private link

On any DJ's row, click Copy portal link — it flips to "Copied!" and the private link to that DJ's personal schedule is on your clipboard. Send it however you like. To remove a DJ, click Delete and confirm.

Your calendar

Creating & managing events

Open Events from the sidebar — your home base, topped with live counts of Upcoming, Past, and Total events.

Create an event

Click + New Event and fill in name, venue, start date, optional end date, and genres. Hit Create.

Clone recurring nights

Running a weekly night? Click Clone to copy an event with its stages but no slots — your setup is ready, just drop in the new lineup.

Edit & delete

Per-row icon buttons let you edit details or delete an event. A green Live badge marks an event happening right now.

The heart of EventLineup

Building the schedule

Click any event to open its detail page — where you set up stages and slot DJs into the lineup. Header buttons let you go Back, View the public schedule, Share via LINE, or Export .xlsx.

Set up stages

A stage is a place where DJs play. Click + New Stage, name it, and pick a color so you can tell stages apart at a glance. Remove one with the × on its chip.

Schedule DJs into slots

Use the inline + New Slot row: pick a Stage and DJ, choose a Genre, set Start time and Duration (presets suggested), add Notes, and save. Search, sort by column, and switch days with date tabs.

Built-in warnings that keep you out of trouble

EventLineup watches for problems as you work and flags them inline:

Double-booking & overlap: "{DJ} is already booked at this time" and "{Stage} already has a set at this time" — the same DJ can't be in two places, and two sets can't share a stage at once.
Certification check: assign a DJ to a genre they aren't certified for and you'll see "{DJ} isn't certified for {genre} — assign anyway to override." This one is a soft warning — you decide.

Confirmation status at a glance

Each DJ's name in the slot table carries a badge showing whether they've responded — and these update automatically when DJs reply through their portal:

✓ Confirmed ⚠ Flagged — Awaiting

Export to Excel: the Export .xlsx button downloads a clean, formatted spreadsheet of the whole lineup (sorted by date, stage, then time) — great for printing, emailing, or keeping a record. Column labels follow your selected language.

For your DJs

The DJ portal

Each DJ gets their own private schedule page. You give access by copying their portal link from the roster and sending it over — no account or password needed. The link is the key.

What the DJ sees

A header reading "Your Schedule," their name and genre tags, then all their sets grouped by event — each card showing date, time, stage, genre, and your notes.

What the DJ can do

  • Confirm — a green check that tells you they're good to go.
  • Flag conflict — let you know there's a problem.
  • Share my card — open a polished promo graphic for that set.
Whatever a DJ chooses flows straight back to your slot table as the Confirmed / Flagged badges. If a link ever expires, just copy a fresh one from the roster and resend it (links last about 90 days).

Get it in front of people

Sharing your event

Public schedule

Anyone with the link can view — no login. A stage-by-stage card view on phones, a time-grid on larger screens. The language toggle works here too.

Share via LINE

Buttons on the event page and public schedule open LINE's composer with the link ready. Sharing is always your choice — EventLineup never messages on its own.

Promo set-cards

A polished, genre-themed promo graphic for each set, with the DJ, stage, date, and venue. Share to LINE, save the image, copy the link, or jump to the full lineup.

Good to know

Frequently asked questions

Do my DJs need to create accounts?

No. You send each DJ a private link, and that's all they need to view their schedule and confirm.

Can the public edit anything?

No. Public and DJ pages are view-and-confirm only. Editing the lineup is something only you (signed in) can do.

What happens if I try to book a DJ twice at the same time?

EventLineup warns you with an amber message and won't let conflicting sets stand on the same stage. You stay in control of how to resolve it.

Can I override a certification warning?

Yes. Certification warnings are advisory — assign the DJ anyway if you decide it's the right call.

Is the app available in Chinese?

Yes — full Traditional Chinese (中文) support across every screen, switchable any time from the top bar.

Can I print or email the schedule?

Yes — use Export .xlsx for a clean spreadsheet, or share the public link / LINE message.
